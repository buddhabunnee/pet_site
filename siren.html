Background
In September of 2011, while I was at work at the shelter, a itty bitty little black kitty was brought in by a good samaritan. She had been found on the side of the highway, along with her dead mother who had been hit by a car. She was so very small, less than a month old. The shelter manager asked me if I could foster her at home for awhile, get her weight up, socialize her, so she might be healthy enough to get a spay surgery and go up for adoption. Here is a picture of her after a few weeks of care, when I brought her back to work to give her her first round of shots. I don't have many pictures of her before this, suffice it to say she resembled an alien and was always covered in her kitten formula or screeching for attention.

Her namesake derives from her behavior in those early months; she was constantly crying out. Whenever she heard any movement of a human she would run up to the edge of her playpen-area and cry out for what seemed like forever. She was incredibly loud! However, in an ironic twist, Siren grew up to be the most stealthy and silent cat of all! She is rarely heard, and when she does feel the need (usually under great pressure) to voice up, it is a very soft and sweet sound.

Personality
Siren is a classic lap cat. If you are sitting or laying down, she is on top of you, somehow. She will find a way to be comfortable anywhere on a human body. She is very much into snuggling into human-made nooks, rubbing her head on hands, purring heavily. Treats? She could take 'em or leave 'em. She isn't nearly as gluttonous as her other roomies. She does love her share of catnip, though, and usually the effects of the 'nip seem to last on her the longest. One thing though, Siren goes crazy for these! Just like her Mommy!
