Background
I first met Scrumba when she was brought to my work at the Humane Society of Missouri in Chesterfield Valley, where I worked for about 3 years in my 20s. She was found as a stray roaming around Ballwin, MO. She was much thinner then, and she was also pregnant when she came to us. Sadly, however, when she gave birth to her kittens while in the shelter, she buried them in her litterbox. She may've done this because she felt the kittens would not survive in her current, stressed-out situation. Maybe it was an accident, or she just wasn't ready for the responsibilities of motherhood.

Either way, she came through and was easily the noisiest cat in the shelter. She had the funniest cry and it was almost constant. It only stopped when I came by and gave her love and attention. Whenever I came by her cage, she got very excited to see me. When I let her out, she didn't attempt to run or hide anywhere, she just wanted to be in my lap, petted and snuggled. I couldn't resist and brought her home before she ever even went out of the adoption floor.

Personality
Scrumba is first and foremost a whiner cry-baby at home. She chirps and cries at nearly anything. Especially adorable is when either my husband or I leave our home, she goes to the door and cries for 3-5 minutes. After that, I think she forgets. Scrumba loves to heavily purr and be petted but only on -her terms- .. she will approach when she is in the 'mood', which is several times a day. Otherwise, she cries and protests at even the slightest touch. It goes without saying that she holds a special place in my heart, as being the first cat I ever adopted. Before I met my husband in 2010, I had never lived with cats. I'm so grateful he introduced me to cats and their funny and sweet ways!
